Thanks to you all for an awesome session! It's incredibly exciting to share physics with pople who are really interested and willing to work hard to understand the material. Even when you don't understand everything, remember that you are way ahead of almost everyone in the world at your age! In any case, nobody understands everything.

Please stay in touch, my email is schillaci@berkeley.edu or cdschillaci@gmail.com. Please also send me your pictures from the last day to remember you all by! If you like I will also post them here for everyone.

Some of you also asked about interesting books to read. Some books my friends recommended:

  • A Brief History of Time by Stephen Hawking
  • Big Bang by Simon Singh
  • Mr. Tompkins in Wonderland or One, Two, Three, Infinity by George Gamow
The book that convinced me physics could be an exciting adventure is Atoms in the Family by Laura Fermi. During winter break from college one year, a storm took our power out for a week. I read this book by the fire and it changed my life!
